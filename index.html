How Medicare Actually Works

The Parts. The Plans.
The Stuff Nobody Explains.

Medicare isn't one thing. It's a system. Here's how it's structured, and where we come in.

Original Medicare: Parts A & B

Part A covers your hospital stays, skilled nursing, hospice, and home health care. Part B covers doctor visits, outpatient care, preventive services, and durable medical equipment. Together, they're the foundation. But they leave gaps. Big ones.

Learn More →

Medicare Advantage: Part C

A private alternative that bundles Parts A, B, and usually Part D into one plan. Often includes dental, vision, and hearing that Original Medicare flat-out doesn't cover. Plans have networks and an annual out-of-pocket maximum, so you always know the worst-case number.

Learn More →

Medicare Supplement (Medigap)

Plugs the holes Original Medicare leaves behind: your 20% coinsurance, deductibles, hospital costs. Any doctor in the U.S. who takes Medicare, no referrals, no networks. Predictable costs. The trade-off is a higher monthly premium. Worth it for a lot of people.

Learn More →

Part D: Prescription Drugs

Because apparently hospital and doctor coverage wasn't complicated enough, drugs are separate. Part D covers your prescriptions. In 2026, your out-of-pocket drug costs are capped at $2,100 for the year. Hit that cap and you pay nothing for covered drugs the rest of the year.

Learn More →

Major Exposures

Here's what most agents skip: Medicare doesn't cover everything. Dental. Vision. Hearing. Long-term care. Accidents. We call these your "exposures." The gaps that exist whether you know about them or not. We walk you through every single one so nothing blindsides you later.
